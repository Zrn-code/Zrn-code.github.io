# Differential and Linear Approximation

# Define

Suppose fff is differentiable at aaa.Then when xxx is closed to aaa.
fff looks like its tangent line y=f(a)+f′(a)(x−a)y = f(a) + f'(a)(x-a)y=f(a)+f′(a)(x−a)and we have f(x)≈f(a)+f′(a)(x−a)f(x) \approx f(a) + f'(a)(x-a)f(x)≈f(a)+f′(a)(x−a).
f(a)+f′(a)(x−a)f(a) + f'(a)(x-a)f(a)+f′(a)(x−a) is called linear approximation of fff at aaa, 記為L(x)=f(a)+f′(a)(x−a)L(x) = f(a) +f'(a)(x-a)L(x)=f(a)+f′(a)(x−a).

# Define

If fff is defined by the equation y=f(x)y = f(x)y=f(x),then the differential of yyy,denoted by dydydy, is given by dy=f′(x)Δxdy = f'(x)\Delta xdy=f′(x)Δx where Δx\Delta xΔx is an arbitrary invrement of xxx.

If fff is defined by the equation y=f(x)y = f(x)y=f(x),then the differential of xxx,denoted by dxdxdx, is given by dx=Δxdx = \Delta xdx=Δx where Δx\Delta xΔx is an arbitrary invrement of xxx.

由以上定義，可得dy=f′(x)dxdy = f'(x)dxdy=f′(x)dx
∵Δy=f(x+Δx)−f(x)\because \Delta y = f(x+\Delta x) -f(x)∵Δy=f(x+Δx)−f(x)(Δy\Delta yΔy is the increment of yyy)
∴Δy≈dy\therefore \Delta y \approx dy∴Δy≈dy when Δx\Delta xΔx is very small.
